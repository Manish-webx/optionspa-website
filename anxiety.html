Understanding the Condition

More Than Just Everyday Stress

Anxiety disorders are medical conditions that go beyond occasional nervousness. They involve persistent, excessive worry that can interfere with work, relationships, and health.

At Options Psychiatry, we understand that anxiety has both biological and psychological roots. Our approach targets the neural pathways responsible for the "fight or flight" response, helping to restore balance and calm.

Early Detection

Early Warning Signs of Anxiety

Recognizing the early signs of anxiety can help you seek treatment sooner, preventing symptoms from escalating and improving your long-term wellness.

Shortness of Breath

Feeling like you can't get enough air or experiencing chest tightness.

Racing Thoughts

A mind that feels "noisy" or uncontrollable worry about future events.

Restlessness

Feeling "keyed up" or unable to sit still and relax.

Digestive Issues

Frequent stomach aches, nausea, or changes in digestion linked to stress.

Know the Signs

How Anxiety Manifests

Anxiety symptoms can be diverse, affecting your body, your emotions, and your cognitive abilities.

Physical

Rapid heart rate, sweating, trembling, muscle tension, and fatigue.

Emotional

Feelings of dread, panic, irritability, or being constantly on edge.

Behavioral

Avoiding social situations, difficulty concentrating, or compulsive behaviors to reduce stress.

Clinical Categories

Common Types of Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety comes in many forms. Understanding your specific diagnosis is the first step toward effective relief.

Generalized Anxiety (GAD)

Persistent and excessive worry about various aspects of daily life, even when there's no clear reason for concern.

Panic Disorder

Characterized by sudden, repeated episodes of intense fear combined with severe physical symptoms.

Social Anxiety Disorder

Intense fear of being watched, judged, or embarrassed in social situations, leading to avoidance.

Specific Phobias

An overwhelming and unreasonable fear of objects or situations that pose little real danger.

OCD

Intrusive thoughts (obsessions) that lead to repetitive behaviors (compulsions) to manage the resulting anxiety.

PTSD

A condition triggered by experiencing or witnessing a terrifying event, causing flashbacks and severe anxiety.
